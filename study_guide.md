## Integrated Revision Plan

| Revision Type          | When & How Often                                       | Action Items |
|------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------|-------------|
| Immediate (Micro)      | After each video/lecture.                              | Pause and summarize the core idea in your own words in your notes; write down one question. |
| Daily (Mini)           | Last 10 mins of each study session.                   | Skim notes from that day; quick mental recap of 2–3 key terms/concepts. |
| Weekly (Core)          | Every Sunday (or Day 7, 14, 21).                      | 1. Create a one-page "cheat sheet" for the completed module. 2. Re-attempt any quiz questions you got wrong. 3. Explain one concept to someone (or on Discord). |
| Pre-Assessment         | Right before each "Assess Your Knowledge" quiz.       | Review your module’s cheat sheet and notes for 15 minutes; treat the quiz as a learning tool. |
| Comprehensive (Macro)  | End of Week 2, and final 2 days after Module 4.       | 1. Review all module cheat sheets. 2. Mind-map connections between modules (e.g., Hardware(M1) → Networking(M2) → Scalability(M3) → Algorithms(M4)). 3. List 5–10 major takeaways. |

## Pro-Tips for Success

- **Active Note-Taking**: Use a dual-column system: Concepts (left) & Examples/Questions (right).
- **Engage on Discord**: Don’t just lurk. Ask your Day 1 question and answer someone else’s; treat it as a learning tool.
- **Hands-On**: For algorithms (Module 4), supplement with free tools like Quirk (circuit simulator) or IBM Composer to visualize concepts.
- **Lab Tours**: Treat these as virtual field trips; note real-world challenges (e.g., cooling, scaling).
- **Health**: Quantum mechanics is intense. Schedule breaks, sleep well, and hydrate. A fresh mind grasps superposition better!

Good luck! You’re embarking on a fantastic journey into the future of computing. Stay consistent and curious.
